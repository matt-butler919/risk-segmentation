Data from Kaggle Auto Insurance Claims Data - https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/buntyshah/auto-insurance-claims-data

A risk segmentation and claim severity analysis divides customers into homogenous groups in order to investigate the cost of individual claims. For this project, I used a publicly available auto insurance claims dataset consisting of 1,000 reported accidents. I built a series of pivot tables and charts segmenting the portfolio by incident type, incident state, insured age, insured sex, and education level to isolate cost drivers across the book. Key findings included that single vehicle collisions and multi-vehicle collisions drove the highest average severities at roughly $64,000 and $62,000 respectively, while parked car incidents and vehicle thefts averaged under $6,000. Geographically, New York and South Carolina incidents trended above average, and North Carolina was below average in terms of severity.
